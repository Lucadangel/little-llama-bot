We design super soft and unique knitwear made only in natural materials — 100% baby alpaca or 80% baby alpaca with 20% silk. ## Washing & Care — Baby Alpaca

Baby alpaca clothing is fantastic and basically does not need to be washed — it can simply be aired out occasionally. Only wash if the item has a stain.

**Hand wash only** — do not machine wash baby alpaca.

- Hand wash in 30°C (hand-warm) water in a dishwashing tub.
- Keep water temperature constant throughout — baby alpaca cannot tolerate temperature changes. Fill the tub first, then add the garment.
- **Detergent:** Use detergent specifically for baby alpaca — must be without lanolin and without enzymes. Little Llama sells suitable detergent. Alternatively, a small teaspoon of organic hair shampoo works.
- Little Llama also sells an organic vegan special soap for baby alpaca knits.
- **Do not tumble dry.** Lay flat to dry in the correct shape.
- **Baby alpaca is hypoallergenic** — ideal for babies with allergies or eczema, as it contains no lanolin and has a smooth, soft, non-itchy surface. It also does not pill.

## Washing & Care — Merino Wool

Merino wool knitwear generally does not need frequent washing — airing out is often enough. Only wash when necessary.

Machine washing is possible only if your machine has a genuine wool programme — but **hand washing is really recommended**.

- **Hand wash preferred:** MAX 30°C (finger-warm/hand-warm) water. Use plenty of water.
- Keep water temperature constant throughout — wool cannot tolerate temperature changes. Fill the tub first, then add the garment.
- **Do not soak** wool knitwear.
- If machine washing, use a **wool programme at max 30°C** and place the garment in a laundry bag.
- Use **liquid wool detergent** with a neutral pH. Little Llama also sells organic, vegan, enzyme-free detergents.
- Move the garment carefully in the water — do not rub.
- Rinse in water at the **same temperature** as the wash water.
- **Drying:** Spin lightly in a pillowcase, or gently press out moisture using towels. **Always dry flat** in the correct shape. Avoid direct sunlight. Never hang a wet wool garment. Avoid terry towels as a drying surface — they absorb moisture and can cause colour bleeding and loss of elasticity.
- **Do not tumble dry** — wool will shrink.
- The surface of wool becomes smoother after washing and light steaming, but **never press wool knitwear** — neither wet nor dry.
- When steaming, use a steam iron or an ordinary iron with a damp cloth between the iron and the garment. **Wool knitwear must never be pressed directly.**
- **Silk must not be pressed or steamed.**
- Hanging wool clothes outside in cold or freezing weather often cleans them naturally (lanolin self-cleans).

Note: Untreated wool, angora, mohair, and alpaca must always be hand washed.

## Washing & Care — Cashmere

Cashmere is one of the finest natural fibres in the world. High-quality cashmere actually becomes softer and nicer with use and washing, and keeps its shape and measurements very well.

Cashmere has a self-cleaning effect — odours disappear naturally, so you do not need to wash it often. Airing outside or hanging in the bathroom while you shower is often enough.

**Machine washing is gentler for cashmere than hand washing** — the machine ensures a constant correct temperature.

**Machine wash:**
- Wash on a safe wool programme at 30°C.
- Place the garment in a laundry bag.
- Wash with similar colours.
- Use a fine wool detergent suitable for cashmere.
- **Do not use fabric softener.**
- **Do not tumble dry.**

**Hand wash (if preferred):**
- Soak in 30°C water for a maximum of 5 minutes — do not soak longer.
- Gently press the wash water through the garment — do not wring or scrub.
- Rinse in water at the same temperature.

**Drying:**
- Lay flat on a towel in the correct shape. Never hang damp or wet cashmere — it will stretch out of shape.
- Stretch gently into shape while still damp.
- Alternatively, roll the garment smoothly in a clean dry towel and leave to dry at room temperature.
- Avoid direct sunlight and strong heat — the garment may bleach.

**Ironing:**
- Cashmere can be ironed with steam on the wool setting (approx. medium heat — 2 dots).
- Always place a towel or cloth between the iron and the cashmere garment.

**Removing stains:**
- Treat stains with a regular wash as described above. Do not scrub or rub stains — this damages cashmere fibres.
- If a stain cannot be removed, contact a dry cleaner.

**Storage:**
- Give your cashmere knit at least one day's rest after wearing.
- Air it out in fresh air for a day occasionally.
- Do not leave cashmere hanging on a hanger for more than a few days — it will stretch out of shape.
- Store cashmere lying flat in a cupboard or drawer.
- Before storing for a season, make sure the garment is clean. Fold in a cotton or plastic bag with cedar or lavender to keep moths away.
- To kill moths and insects, place the garment in a sealed freezer bag in the freezer for a day.

**Pilling (lint/fluff):**
- All cashmere knitwear pills initially, especially under the arms and where knit rubs against knit. This is normal and decreases with use and washing — high-quality cashmere consists mainly of long fibres that pill less than short fibres.
- Remove pills with a wool comb (pull in one direction on a flat surface), carefully by hand, or with an electric lint remover (use carefully on loosely knitted items).
- Remove pills before washing — the garment will feel softer and fluffier after washing.
